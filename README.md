## What is VentureOS?

VentureOS is an open-source, local-first Business Operating System designed to discover, validate, build, market, and continuously improve software ventures.

It is not simply another AI assistant, multi-agent framework, or research tool.

It is an evidence-driven organisational intelligence platform.

Rather than asking AI to generate answers, VentureOS builds a structured organisation that researches opportunities, challenges assumptions, records decisions, runs experiments, preserves knowledge, and learns from outcomes.

The goal is not to replace human judgement.

The goal is to augment it through disciplined reasoning, transparent evidence, constructive debate, and organisational memory.

---

# Why VentureOS Exists

Building successful software businesses is difficult.

Good ideas are plentiful.

Good decisions are rare.

Most ventures fail because of poor assumptions, insufficient evidence, lack of validation, weak marketing, poor execution, or failure to learn from previous mistakes.

Current AI tools are excellent at generating content.

Few are designed to generate organisational knowledge.

VentureOS exists to change that.

---

# The Philosophy

VentureOS is founded on four simple ideas.

> **Ideas are hypotheses.**

> **Evidence is currency.**

> **Decisions are experiments.**

> **Learning is the only permanent asset.**

Every feature, workflow, and agent within VentureOS exists to reinforce these principles.

# The Organisation

VentureOS is designed as a collection of collaborating departments rather than isolated AI agents.

Examples include:

* Research
* Marketing
* Product
* Development
* Finance
* Legal
* Customer Success
* Board
* Meta Intelligence

Each department contributes evidence, analysis, and recommendations to shared cases.

No single agent owns the truth.

---

# Cases

Everything inside VentureOS revolves around Cases.

A Case represents a venture, idea, project, architectural decision, or strategic investigation.

Each Case contains:

* observations
* evidence
* hypotheses
* assumptions
* opportunities
* risks
* debates
* decisions
* experiments
* outcomes
* lessons
* assets
* metrics

Cases become the permanent memory of the organisation.

---

# Decision Making

Every significant decision must be:

* evidenced
* challenged
* recorded
* reviewable
* traceable

Every important decision should answer:

* Why was it made?
* What evidence supported it?
* What objections were raised?
* What alternatives were considered?
* What happened afterwards?

If VentureOS cannot explain a decision, it should not make it.

---

# Self Improvement

VentureOS is intended to improve itself using the same governance it applies to every other venture.

The system may propose improvements.

Those proposals must be supported by evidence.

They are reviewed by the Board.

Major changes require human approval.

VentureOS should never evolve through opaque or unreviewed changes.

---

# Local First

The core platform is designed to operate entirely on open-source software.

Where practical, the platform should avoid dependence on proprietary APIs.

Preferred technologies include:

* Ollama
* Open-source language models
* Open-source embedding models
* Self-hosted databases
* Open-source orchestration
* Open-source vector search
* Local document processing
* Open-source web research

Cloud services should always remain optional.

# This Repository

This repository is intentionally organised around organisational knowledge rather than code.

The documentation is considered as important as the implementation.

Major architectural decisions are recorded.

Research is preserved.

Cases are tracked.

Lessons are retained.

Knowledge accumulates.

---

# VentureOS Is Its Own First Case

The first venture managed by VentureOS is VentureOS itself.

Every architectural decision, assumption, experiment, debate, and lesson should be recorded using the same principles that will later be applied to every other venture.

If VentureOS cannot successfully guide its own development, it has no authority to guide anyone else's.
