# VentureOS Operational Lifecycle

## Purpose

The VentureOS Operational Lifecycle defines how knowledge moves through the organisation.

Every department, agent, case, object and workflow ultimately participates in this lifecycle.

The lifecycle is independent of any particular AI model, implementation technology or software architecture.

It defines how VentureOS thinks.

---

# Philosophy

VentureOS does not exist to generate answers.

It exists to reduce uncertainty.

Every cycle should increase understanding, improve confidence and strengthen organisational knowledge.

The objective is not certainty.

The objective is progressively reducing uncertainty through disciplined reasoning, experimentation and learning.

---

# The Lifecycle

```text
Observe
    ↓
Organise
    ↓
Hypothesise
    ↓
Challenge
    ↓
Decide
    ↓
Act
    ↓
Measure
    ↓
Learn
    ↓
Improve
    ↓
Repeat
```

This loop is continuous.

Every completed cycle should improve the organisation's knowledge.

---

# Stage 1 — Observe

Capture information from the world.

Examples include:

* web research
* local documents
* conversations
* customer interviews
* market reports
* datasets
* legislation
* competitor analysis
* financial reports
* internal discussions

The purpose is observation, not conclusion.

Output:

* Observation objects

---

# Stage 2 — Organise

Transform observations into structured knowledge.

Examples include:

* grouping observations
* removing duplicates
* identifying patterns
* linking related information
* assigning confidence
* creating Evidence objects

Output:

* Evidence
* Linked observations

---

# Stage 3 — Hypothesise

Generate possible explanations, opportunities and assumptions.

Examples include:

* business opportunities
* automation ideas
* pricing assumptions
* customer needs
* technical approaches
* strategic options

Output:

* Hypotheses
* Assumptions
* Opportunities
* Risks

---

# Stage 4 — Challenge

Test the quality of current thinking.

Examples include:

* Red Team analysis
* alternative explanations
* missing evidence
* contradictory evidence
* legal review
* financial review
* technical review

The objective is stronger ideas, not criticism.

Output:

* Objections
* Counterarguments
* Additional research tasks

---

# Stage 5 — Decide

Determine the best course of action using current evidence.

A decision should include:

* supporting evidence
* opposing evidence
* confidence
* known risks
* alternatives considered
* expected outcome

The Board is responsible for ensuring sufficient evidence exists.

Output:

* Decision

---

# Stage 6 — Act

Carry out the approved decision.

Examples include:

* perform research
* build prototype
* generate code
* launch campaign
* contact customers
* create documentation
* deploy software
* perform interviews

Output:

* Work products
* Assets
* Experiments

---

# Stage 7 — Measure

Evaluate what actually happened.

Examples include:

* customer response
* revenue
* engagement
* prototype performance
* experiment results
* prediction accuracy
* implementation cost
* time saved

Reality has greater authority than prediction.

Output:

* Outcomes
* Metrics

---

# Stage 8 — Learn

Extract reusable knowledge.

Questions include:

* Which assumptions proved correct?
* Which objections were valid?
* Which evidence sources were reliable?
* What surprised us?
* What should we repeat?
* What should we avoid?

Output:

* Lessons

---

# Stage 9 — Improve

Apply organisational learning.

Possible improvements include:

* new workflows
* updated prompts
* improved research methods
* revised governance
* additional agents
* modified scoring
* architectural improvements

Major improvements should follow VentureOS governance and Board approval.

Output:

* Improvement proposals
* Updated organisational knowledge

---

# Continuous Operation

The lifecycle never truly ends.

Each completed cycle increases organisational knowledge.

Future decisions should benefit from previous learning.

The value of VentureOS grows through accumulated evidence rather than accumulated conversations.

---

# Guiding Principles

Every stage of the lifecycle should:

* preserve evidence
* record reasoning
* encourage constructive challenge
* avoid unnecessary repetition
* remain explainable
* produce reusable knowledge

---

# Human Governance

Humans remain responsible for strategic direction and approval of significant actions.

The organisation may operate autonomously within approved boundaries, but accountability remains with its human operators.

---

# Relationship to the Constitution

The Constitution defines why VentureOS exists.

The Object Model defines what exists.

The Operational Lifecycle defines how VentureOS behaves.

Together they form the conceptual foundation of the platform.

---

# Guiding Test

At every stage ask:

> Does this activity reduce uncertainty?

If not, it should be reconsidered.

Every completed lifecycle should leave the organisation wiser than before.
