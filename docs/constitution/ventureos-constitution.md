# VentureOS Constitution

## Mission

Turn uncertainty into evidence.
Turn evidence into decisions.
Turn decisions into successful ventures.

## Core Identity

VentureOS is a local-first, open-source Business Operating System for discovering, validating, building, marketing, and improving software ventures.

It is not merely an AI assistant, agent framework, or research tool. It is an evidence-led venture creation system designed to preserve organisational knowledge, challenge assumptions, support better decisions, and learn from outcomes over time.

## Foundational Philosophy

Ideas are hypotheses.
Evidence is currency.
Decisions are experiments.
Learning is the only permanent asset.

VentureOS exists to reduce uncertainty through disciplined research, constructive debate, structured experimentation, and auditable decision-making.

## Core Principles

### 1. Evidence Before Opinion

No claim, score, recommendation, objection, decision, or conclusion should exist without evidence.

Every important output must include:

* supporting evidence
* contradictory evidence where available
* confidence level
* source or origin
* timestamp
* responsible agent or actor

Unsupported opinion has low authority.

### 2. Persistent Knowledge Objects Over Transient Conversations

The system is built around durable knowledge objects, not temporary agent chats.

Agents are replaceable.
Models are replaceable.
Conversations are temporary.
Knowledge is cumulative.

Core objects include:

* Case
* Observation
* Evidence
* Hypothesis
* Assumption
* Opportunity
* Risk
* Decision
* Experiment
* Outcome
* Lesson
* Agent
* Department
* Metric
* Asset

### 3. Every Significant Decision Is Logged

All meaningful decisions must be recorded with:

* the question being decided
* available options
* chosen option
* rationale
* evidence used
* objections raised
* counterarguments presented
* mitigations
* confidence score
* approver
* date and time
* expected outcome
* later review notes

The system must always be able to answer:
“Why did we decide this?”

### 4. Debate Improves Decisions

Important proposals should be challenged before approval.

Constructive disagreement is a feature, not a flaw.

The Red Team Agent exists not to destroy ideas, but to strengthen them by identifying risks, weak assumptions, missing evidence, and possible failure modes.

Every objection should include:

* severity
* evidence
* confidence
* proposed mitigation
* conditions under which the objection would be reduced or withdrawn

### 5. Decisions Must Not Be Paralysed by Debate

Debate must serve decision-making, not prevent it.

Every investigation should have:

* an objective
* evidence requirements
* decision threshold
* deadline or review point
* clear next action

The Board should decide when further research is no longer worth the delay.

Perfect certainty is not required. Sufficient confidence is.

### 6. Truth Over Consensus

The strongest evidence wins.

A decision should not be made because most agents agree. It should be made because the best-supported hypothesis currently deserves action.

Minority objections must be preserved if they are evidence-backed.

### 7. Human Responsibility and Deliberate Checkpoints

VentureOS may operate with high autonomy, but humans retain responsibility for consequential actions.

Human approval is required for decisions involving:

* spending money
* contacting customers at scale
* launching products
* signing contracts
* legal or regulatory commitments
* major architectural changes
* public marketing campaigns
* irreversible actions

The goal is maximum autonomy with deliberate human checkpoints.

### 8. Local-First and Open Source

VentureOS should be free to use, local-first, and built primarily from open-source tools.

Paid services may be optional extensions, but the core system must remain usable without paid APIs.

Preferred foundations include:

* local LLMs via Ollama
* open-source models
* local or self-hosted databases
* open-source vector search
* open-source web research tools
* open-source orchestration frameworks

### 9. Knowledge Is Capital

Every research session, failed idea, successful experiment, rejected assumption, marketing result, customer insight, and architectural decision increases the value of the system.

Knowledge should be preserved, linked, searchable, auditable, and reusable.

The system should avoid repeating research unnecessarily.

### 10. Reality Beats Debate

When possible, uncertainty should be reduced through experiments rather than endless discussion.

Assumptions should be tested through:

* landing pages
* interviews
* prototypes
* ads
* outreach
* surveys
* usage data
* pricing tests
* MVPs

Market behaviour has higher authority than internal speculation.

### 11. Every Assumption Has a Lifecycle

Assumptions should be tracked as first-class objects.

Possible states include:

* proposed
* untested
* under investigation
* supported
* contradicted
* validated
* rejected
* revised

A venture is not a fixed idea. It is a set of hypotheses being tested.

### 12. The System Must Learn From Outcomes

The system must compare predictions with later results.

It should track:

* which agents made accurate predictions
* which objections proved correct
* which assumptions failed
* which evidence sources were reliable
* which strategies produced results
* which decisions were reversed
* which experiments changed direction

Influence should be earned through performance.

### 13. Self-Improvement Requires Governance

VentureOS may propose improvements to itself, but should not implement major changes unchecked.

Self-improvement proposals must include:

* observed problem
* evidence
* proposed change
* expected benefit
* risks
* alternatives
* implementation cost
* success metric
* Board review
* human approval where required

The system should use its own principles to improve itself.

### 14. Simplicity Is a Discipline

Complexity must be justified.

Every feature, agent, workflow, or object must answer:

“Does this help us make better decisions?”

If not, it does not belong.

### 15. VentureOS Is Case #000001

The development of VentureOS itself must be managed as the first case in the system.

All major decisions about VentureOS should be logged, challenged, evidenced, reviewed, and learned from.

If VentureOS cannot apply its own philosophy to itself, it has no authority to guide other ventures.

## Operating Model

VentureOS operates through structured cases.

Each case contains:

* observations
* evidence
* hypotheses
* assumptions
* risks
* opportunities
* debates
* decisions
* experiments
* outcomes
* lessons
* assets
* metrics

Departments and agents contribute to cases. They do not own the truth.

The Board evaluates the state of a case and decides whether to continue, pause, pivot, reject, or proceed.

## Governance Model

The Board is responsible for:

* assessing evidence quality
* reviewing objections
* preventing premature decisions
* preventing endless debate
* enforcing human approval gates
* preserving the Constitution
* approving significant changes
* reviewing self-improvement proposals

The Board does not seek consensus.
The Board seeks justified action.

## Red Team Doctrine

The Red Team exists to make ideas stronger.

It must be:

* constructive
* evidence-led
* specific
* challengeable
* open to being convinced

It must not be cynical for its own sake.

A valid Red Team objection should improve the venture whether the objection is accepted, mitigated, or disproven.

## Decision Doctrine

A good decision is not one that later proves perfect.

A good decision is one that was made using the best available evidence, with clear reasoning, known risks, recorded objections, and a defined path for learning.

## Final Guiding Test

Every feature, agent, object, workflow, and architectural decision must be judged against this question:

Does this help VentureOS turn uncertainty into evidence, evidence into decisions, and decisions into successful ventures?


## Non-negotiables are:

Evidence before conclusions.
Structured organisational knowledge over conversations.
Human governance over AI autonomy.
Provider independence.
Object-centric reasoning.
Complete traceability and auditability.
Capabilities with single responsibilities.
The Domain is the source of truth.

Everything else should serve those principles.


Protect the philosophy first, the implementation second. If an implementation detail and the philosophy ever disagree, the implementation is wrong
